What is PĀUA?

PĀUA is an initiative that aims to raise the importance of cultural competency and awareness across New Zealand professional firms. It connects passionate culturally diverse students with like-minded New Zealand professionals.


Research shows that diverse candidates often face significant challenges and barriers in accessing mentoring, forming networks and gaining exposure within the professional services sector. PĀUA aims to overcome this, through facilitating culturally-focused mentoring.



The Why?

There is a need for better cultural competency, understanding and representation within firms and organisations in New Zealand.


Individuals should feel confident and comfortable to embrace and share their cultural backgrounds, stories and experiences within their workplaces.


To achieve that, peers and leaders must foster an environment of cultural awareness and understanding. PĀUA aims to achieve that through cultural communication between young and experienced professionals.

The Purpose

With an aim to empower students, PĀUA partnered mentors will provide professional and pastoral advice for PĀUA mentees.

By 2021, we aim to see a generation of PĀUA students prosper through the programme and make proactive change in the cultural landscape of New Zealand's professional sectors.

Programme Structure


The structure of the programme will be implemented on a 3;1 mentee to mentor ratio basis. Mentees will have the opportunity to be mentored throughout the programme by a mentor. Each mentor is expected to meet with their mentees for a minimum of 3 sessions during the programme.
